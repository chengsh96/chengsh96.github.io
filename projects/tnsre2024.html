Continuous Control of Inter-Leg Transitions Across Activities (TNSRE 2024)

Best Student Paper · IROS 2023
Research
TNSRE 2024 Paper IROS 2023 Paper Watch Video

This project enables smooth, biomimetic transitions between level walking and stair ascent/descent for a powered knee–ankle prosthesis—without relying on discrete mode switching. It supports all eight inter-leg transition scenarios (ascent vs. descent, to vs. from stairs, and intact-led vs. prosthetic-led transitions), which is critical for natural, real-world mobility.

Award: This work received the 2023 IEEE/RSJ IROS Best Student Paper Award.

What I built

  • A continuously-varying transition (CVT) trajectory model that smoothly changes joint kinematics across the entire gait cycle during transitions, reducing discontinuities and “controller surprises.”
  • An optimal-switching transition (OST) baseline that discretely switches between steady-state models at an optimized gait phase—used as a strong comparison point against CVT.
  • A phase-variable control framework that executes these models in real time, enabling uninterrupted, phase-aligned trajectory generation for both knee and ankle.
  • A robustness-to-delay analysis showing why continuous parameterization helps when intent classification is delayed or imperfect—one of the major practical failure modes in mode-switching systems.

Why it matters

  • Eliminates brittle switching timing: switching-based controllers depend heavily on “perfect” transition timing; CVT reduces sensitivity to timing errors.
  • Biomimetic across inter-leg cases: transitions led by the intact vs. prosthetic leg can have distinct biomechanics; this framework explicitly handles those differences.
  • Experimental validation: demonstrated seamless transitions across all eight scenarios with transfemoral amputee participants and two stair/step-height configurations.
