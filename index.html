About

Research → deployment

I make wearable robots less robotic.

My work blends control, embedded systems, and machine learning to help robotic devices understand human movement intent in milliseconds. I focus on taking algorithms from simulation to production — where safety, reliability, and real-world messiness matter most.

I like building systems that feel simple to the user because the engineering underneath is anything but.

The Hidden Engineering in a Step

Tap a phase to see what the robot thinks.

A single step contains more information than it seems. For wearable robots, each stride is a stream of clues: intent, terrain, balance, timing, and safety. My work focuses on turning those clues into real-time control decisions that feel natural outside the lab.

01

Sense

Read motion, contact, load, and environment cues.

02

Estimate

Infer gait phase, intent, terrain, and timing.

03

Assist

Apply support at the right moment under real-time limits.

04

Recover

Prioritize stability, safety, and the next stride.

Human

The foot contacts the ground. Body weight begins transferring onto the leading leg.

Robot senses

Impact timing, rapid force rise, foot orientation, and angular velocity from the IMU.

Controller asks

"Has stance begun? Is this a normal step, a sudden stop, or an uneven surface?"
